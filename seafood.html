Our Business

At Oceanda, we specialize in physical commodity trading, particularly in high-quality seafood sourced from Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Cameroon, Ivory Coast). We deal in a wide range of seafood products, including shrimp, lobster, tilapia, catfish, mackerel, and crabs, all sustainably harvested from local waters. Our seafood is naturally farmed or caught, ensuring it meets non-GMO standards and is free of harmful chemicals. We source, process, and export seafood in large volumes, meeting the exacting demands of our global clients

SeaFood Products

Prawn

Prawn is a common name for small aquatic crustaceans with an exoskeleton and ten legs (members of the order of decapods). We deal with prawns freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried. Varieties include the Giant Tiger Prawn, also known as the Black Tiger (Penaeus monodon), Brown Prawns, etc.

Shinnose

Shinnose, or Shinnose Snapper, refers to a group of fish primarily found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. These fish are known for their vibrant colors, often featuring bright pink or red hues. They inhabit deep waters, preferring rocky substrates and coral reefs. Shinnose are prized for their firm, white flesh and are popular in both commercial and recreational fishing. We deal with Shinnose Snapper freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried

Barracuda

Barracuda, any of about 20 species of predacious fishes of the family Sphyraenidae (order Perciformes). Barracudas are found in all warm and tropical regions; some also range into more temperate areas. Swift and powerful, they are slender in form, with small scales, two well-separated dorsal fins, a jutting lower jaw, and a large mouth with many large, sharp teeth. We deal with barracuda freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Red Snapper

Red Snapper is a highly sought-after fish in the family Lutjanidae, primarily found in warm waters of the Atlantic and Gulf of Mexico. They are recognized for their distinctive reddish skin and a mild, sweet flavor. These fish inhabit rocky bottoms and coral reefs and are popular among anglers. Red Snapper is often grilled, baked, or fried and is a staple in many seafood dishes. We deal with Red Snapper freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Yellowtail

Yellowtail, specifically the Yellowtail Amberjack, is a fast-swimming fish found in the Pacific Ocean. Characterized by its elongated body and bright yellow stripe along its side, it is a favorite among sport fishermen. Yellowtail is known for its rich flavor and firm texture, making it a popular choice for sushi and sashimi. These fish often form large schools and are commonly found near offshore structures. We deal with Yellowtail freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Mackerel

Mackerel refers to several species in the family Scombridae, commonly found in temperate and tropical waters worldwide. These fish are known for their streamlined bodies and distinct vertical stripes. Mackerel are fast swimmers, feeding on small fish and plankton. They have a rich, oily flesh, making them ideal for grilling, smoking, or canning. Their high omega-3 fatty acid content makes them a nutritious choice. We deal with mackerel freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Crabs

Crabs are a diverse group of crustaceans belonging to the order Decapoda, with over 6,800 species found worldwide. They are typically found in oceans, freshwater, and on land. Crabs are known for their hard exoskeleton, five pairs of legs, and large pincers. Popular species like Blue Crab and Dungeness Crab are prized for their sweet, delicate meat, often steamed or used in various dishes such as crab cakes and soups.

Octopus

Octopus, another cephalopod, is recognized by its eight arms lined with suction cups and high intelligence. They inhabit ocean floors worldwide, camouflaging themselves in rocky crevices. Octopus meat is firm, with a slightly chewy texture and mild flavor, often grilled or boiled in Mediterranean and Asian cuisines. It is a popular ingredient in dishes such as sushi, takoyaki, and salads, appreciated for its rich taste and versatility. We deal with octopus freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Crayfish

Crayfish, also known as crawfish or crawdads, are freshwater crustaceans closely related to lobsters. Found in streams, rivers, and swamps, crayfish are especially popular in Southern U.S. cuisine, particularly in Cajun and Creole dishes. These small creatures have tender, sweet meat, usually boiled with seasonings and served in seafood boils. Crayfish are highly adaptable and are a staple in various regional dishes around the world. We deal with crayfish freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.

Squid

Squid, belonging to the class Cephalopoda, are soft-bodied marine animals known for their elongated bodies and tentacles. They are found in oceans worldwide and are known for their ability to swim quickly using jet propulsion. Calamari refers to squid prepared as food, usually sliced into rings and fried. Squid meat is tender with a mild flavor, and it is used in a variety of dishes, from grilled preparations to stir-fries and stews. We deal with squid freshly caught from the sea in their varieties, client volumes, and process type, be it fresh or dried.
