Solid Minerals

Our Business

At Oceanda, we specialize in physical commodity trading, with a focus on high-quality solid minerals sourced from Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Cameroon, Ivory Coast). We deal in a wide range of solid minerals, including gold, bauxite, limestone, tin, zinc, and granite, all extracted sustainably from rich mineral deposits. Our minerals meet industry standards and are free from harmful contaminants. We source, process, and export these minerals in large volumes, ensuring that we meet the specific needs of our global clients with precision and reliability.

Solid Mineral Products

Lithium

Lithium is a highly sought-after mineral, particularly due to its critical role in the production of rechargeable batteries for electric vehicles, smartphones, and other electronics. It is a lightweight metal known for its electrochemical potential, making it essential for the renewable energy sector and energy storage technologies.

Gold

Gold is a precious metal valued for its rarity and beauty. It has been used for centuries in jewelry, currency, and as a safe investment. Beyond its ornamental and financial uses, gold has industrial applications in electronics and dentistry due to its conductivity and resistance to corrosion.

Iron

Iron ore is the primary raw material used to produce steel, a fundamental building block in infrastructure, automotive, and manufacturing industries. With large deposits found in Africa, iron ore is critical for global development, making it one of the most widely traded minerals worldwide.

Bitumen

Bitumen, commonly known as asphalt, is a sticky, black, and highly viscous liquid or semi-solid form of petroleum. It is mainly used in road construction for paving and as a binder mixed with aggregate particles to create asphalt concrete. Bitumen is also used in waterproofing products and roofing.

Coal

Coal is a combustible black or brownish-black sedimentary rock that is primarily used as a fuel source for electricity generation. It also has applications in steel production (coking coal) and as a key energy source for various industries. Coal remains one of the most abundant and economical sources of energy globally.

Talc

Talc is a soft mineral commonly used in the production of talcum powder, cosmetics, and personal care products due to its moisture-absorbing and anti-friction properties. It also finds applications in the paper, plastics, and ceramics industries as a filler and coating material.
